☕ Project Overview
This project is an interactive Excel dashboard designed to analyze the sales and profit performance of a multi-state coffee chain. It provides a comprehensive view of financial KPIs, product trends, and regional performance, allowing stakeholders to make data-driven decisions regarding inventory and expansion.

🎯 Purpose & Objectives
The primary goal of this dashboard is to track the health of the coffee chain across different territories. It answers key business questions such as:

Which products are driving the highest profit margins?

What are the peak sales months throughout the year?

How do different territories compare in terms of market size and sales volume?

Which specific states/cities are the top performers?

🛠️ Tech Stack
Microsoft Excel: Core platform for data processing and visualization.

Power Query: (If used) For data cleaning and transformation.

Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts: For dynamic data summarization.

Slicers: For interactive filtering by Year and Product Type.

✨ Key Features & Highlights
KPI Scorecard: Instant visibility into total Sales (819k+), Profit (259k+), Expenses (229k+), and Inventory (3.1M+).

Time-Series Analysis: A line chart showing the Sales Trend across the months, highlighting seasonal peaks (e.g., the significant jump in July).

Product Performance: A dual-line chart comparing Sum of Profit vs. Sum of Sales per product, identifying high-revenue stars like Colombian and Lemon.

Geographic Insights: * Top 10 Cities: A ranked bar chart showing California as the leading market.

Territory Trend: A grouped bar chart comparing Major vs. Small Territories across East, West, South, and Central regions.

Interactive Filters: Dynamic slicers for Year (2012-2013) and Product Categories (Beans vs. Leaves) to drill down into specific data segments.

📊 Business Insights Derived
Profitability Gap: While "Lemon" shows a massive spike in sales, its profit margin follows closely, indicating healthy pricing.

Regional Dominance: The West region shows the highest sales in small territories, while the Central region dominates in major territories.

Inventory Management: With inventory levels at over 3 million units, the dashboard suggests a need to correlate stock levels with the sales dips seen in November.

How to Use the Dashboard
Filter by Year: Use the top-left slicer to toggle between 2012 and 2013 data.

Filter by Product: Select "Beas..." (Beans) or "Lea..." (Leaves) to see category-specific trends.

Hover & Analyze: Review the trend lines to identify specific months or products that require operational attention.
